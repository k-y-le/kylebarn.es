and i’m returning to my 2026 vision board, thinking about futures and pasts and all the ways i’m becoming someone new, all the time. for some reason, time feels particularly slippery this year, all rush and flow and whirling water. i don’t mind it; i’m practicing. perhaps it is, like mckenzie wark suggests, “a sideways time, without memory or expectations, without history or desire.” life practice, in the “how are you?” call and response. how do we mark our timescapes? injury, victory, seismic social shifts.

i love the phrase big sky. because the sky is always the same size, right? it’s only the subjective gaze that shifts. my gaze rests upon the gray cloudy day, and the sky feels small, cramped. summer humidity snaking through my life. lately i’ve been craving big sky, rhythms of flatness, great expanse and summer thunderstorm on the horizon. big blue truck, big blue sky.

instead i’m here in the big blue city, electric in its own way. sports has come to town, leisure takes center stage. knicks in five and i shed a tear for a team i never really cared much about. so much of the way older folks tend to tell time has to do with milestones of favorite teams, or favored politicians. where were you when obama was elected? when the cubs won the world series? we crave belonging, effervescence, watershed moments. triumph.

what to do when time slips between our fingers, despite our best efforts to catch it? here’s anaïs nin: “We do not grow absolutely, chronologically. We grow sometimes in one dimension, and not in another; unevenly. We grow partially. We are relative. We are mature in one realm, childish in another. The past, present, and future mingle and pull us backward, forward, or fix us in the present. We are made up of layers, cells, constellations.” as i’ve grown into the flow, let the water wash past me, i feel other parts of myself pass me by. all ends, eventually, but more interestingly, all reconfigures and reshapes.

also, the summer. the sweat and swelter. today i’m preparing to step into a week of constant motion, for the summer of ludd, cautiously eyeing the forthcoming heat wave. again, i’m practicing. letting it drip off of me, some salty cleanse. there’s something to the sharp crags in a winter sky that allows memory to snag, whereas in summer it’s all slog. i’m feeling this, too, feeling into it. finding refuge.
