on sunday, i’m laying on the hot pavement telling johnny about my dreams. waxing poetic about the value of the absurd to humble our hubris, i look up to see rising above me a giant paper mache face. it’s lady ludd, who earlier nearly toppled amber following a sweeping gust of wind. i’ve been watching my friends perform a play in public, gently urging strangers to put down their phones when they stop to take a video, going “hey, i think i know you…” to dozens of vaguely familiar faces. 

come monday, and i munch on a burrito while maya and miles lead a brazilian circle singing kickoff for a jamboree, tapping my foot before jumping in. giving hugs to the many friends nearby, i make my way to a community garden where i distribute business cards i had just picked up with a url for a web page i just created. over the course of the week, i’ll see my moon bulletin nearly double in subscriber count, from ~200 to ~400, alongside its first mention in local news. but i have to leave the calendar event to frantically bike across the manhattan bridge back into brooklyn, stopping by home for just a second to put on my red pants and rush on over to fort greene park for the strawberry moon ritual. i set up in one corner before wini calls me, says there’s 25 people waiting at the monument. i gather my things and beckon them to come sit under a tree, and soon 25 turns to 40 turns to 60 and keeps growing even as the ritual begins.  some passersby join, and for the first time ever the moon ritual is more strangers than friends. but this week it feels like the spectrum from stranger to friend is all skewed, elliptical as i begin to see the same faces again and again.

tuesday afternoon and i rearrange my schedule to make it to janet’s teach-in. i’m skipping the conference to see her before she’s off to canada. i have to leave the poetry reading early, but i make it to dan’s presidency announcement. then back to tompkins square park, where just an hour later i’m standing atop a water cooler, orchestrating nearly a hundred rats in human bodies as they scurry back and forth. between fielding questions about rat control, behavior, and anatomy, i take a moment to wonder at the fact that — were it not for the assembled crowd — i would surely strike a different impression in my state, yelling rat facts into the public square. who’s here? a question i’ll ask all week. tonight it’s passersby, curious luddites, and — let me not forget — the rat aficionados. i leave the evening with an invite to a play, an offer to teach a workshop at an art school, and a request to be in a documentary.

wednesday i’m handing out leaf zines and expounding upon the difference between palmate and pinnate venation while temperatures approach triple digits. we rest with misters, shade, breaks under the leaves we tell stories about. but once it’s over i can hardly rest, as i head to the grass to lead a teach-in about the cyborg manifesto. i’m looking forward to giving my voice a break, after days of yelling to big crowds, anticipating that the heat and niche feminist topic will only appeal to a few. for not the first time this week, i’m underestimating; over sixty people gather around, at attention while i read them marxist feminist theory from the 80s, lead a phone meditation, fail to effectively explain what the word “oedipal” means. something’s happening here; i’m feeling in the response to my events this week something i failed to ever receive despite the enticing promises of social media platforms. this is social infrastructure at work; what started as a clever idea became a group of friends meeting in a church became a crowd gathering on the hottest week of the year for something they only heard about in real life.

but we’re not done yet; thursday i’m sheltering from the sweltering with nick in the quaker meeting house. we’ve assembled an altar of ephemera from the week — zines, books, pamphlets, a drawing of me someone did during my teach-in. and after nina leads a quick meditation, we use the space of the computer care café to look forwards, towards integration of the freeing week of luddite activities into our daily lives, towards a care relationship with our devices. between bursts of compressed air, we talk about alternatives to big tech platforms, scheduling regular time to clean our folders up, and reconfiguring our relationship to tech, collectively. the single window AC tries its very best to keep the growing group cool, and stepping back into the wall of heat outside afterwards i feel silently grateful to be inside, even if i had to miss one of the bread and puppet shows happening in the park. 

friday morning after yoga i catch up on various tasks, while taking scattered calls and sending messages in preparation for one of the biggest undertakings of the week: the third edition of let’s get off together. volunteers gather gradually in the leadup to doors opening, and i’m filled with gratitude at all the wonderful people coming together to make this little dream into a reality for the third time. it’s been a week of changing perspective, and for me that means shifting from previous anxiety about getting enough interest to break even, and towards the newest concerns popping up — namely, the venue going over capacity. i acutely feel the responsibilities of party host as i monitor the space for partygoers overheating and try to kindly yet forcefully herd folks off the roof that’s nearly buckling under the weight of the crowd. somehow we meet each challenge where its at and i finish the night with a dj set i had zero time to practice, where despite the heat a crowd continues to jam out, and i continue to relish in the feeling of connectivity even as i fall asleep post-sunrise.

saturday i give myself the gift of rest, yawning my way through a casual hang with my roommates and some friends before ending up at the beach for a kookout, where i offer some games and some ritual to a growing crew of festival-goers. mostly, it’s just magical being at the beach while fireworks burst at random intervals throughout the night, made doubly so by the social permission our space offers — permission to chase each other stealing gnome hats, to play duck duck goose, to yell “end the american empire!” into the sand. we’re learning to go moment to moment, like when the A stops running and a group of us trek over a mile as the rain pours down, walking in the middle of the road towards a bus stop that might take us back to brooklyn. 

and then we practice saying goodbye on sunday, amalia amber and nick leading us through a closing ceremony, where we remember the moments of the week (i’m honored to hear a couple strangers share being touched by gatherings of mine), before running out through the park to try (mostly unsuccessfully) to get strangers to delete a social media account. then we’ve got another rendition of the play, but this time i take my leave and head home to watch the sky change from my bedroom window, relishing in my aloneness after a week of constant activity. 

this was the summer of ludd, a week of free public participatory programming centered in tompkins square park. we were gathering people in public space, off big tech, building alternative social infrastructure in an offline manner. it was an experiment — will people come to an event they only heard about irl? (yes) will we have fun? (yes) can we take over public space even in a heat wave? (yes). and i learned so much along the way, about flexibility, performance, projecting my voice, rats, cyborgs, hosting an overfull warehouse rave on the hottest day of the year.
