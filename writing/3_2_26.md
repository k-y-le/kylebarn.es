my notes app todo list this week is titled “and so the tendrils of spring begin to arrive within me.” i saw my first glimpse of dandelion shoots while birding this past saturday morning. it was a bright and cheerful time, watching the water fowl parade through the few melted corners of prospect park lake.

patterns in my mood match closely with how connected i feel to my body. embodiment can sometimes be a bit of a brooklyn artist buzzword, but i think that’s because we’re all alighting on something important. feeling connected to your body, to the land around you, is so so valuable, especially when we have a tendency to be energetically siphoned into our screens. i’m trying to weave this into each thing i do, from moon parties to food pop-ups to organizing meetings to silly parties. and i hope that, in doing so, i cocoon myself in a sustaining practice of connection and joy. basically i want to be everyone’s embodiment/ritual/nature friend!

at an ecosomatic movement workshop this past weekend (yes you read that right… this is my artful life and i’m living it…), we started by writing our desires on a book cover with the pages ripped out. then, we ended by sharing those desires, being witnessed by others however we wanted it. it felt special to share my wants with a group of strangers on my own terms, and so i wanted to share it here, with a group of not-so-strangers :). also, an invitation — should it feel alive to you, i welcome any desires that you might be harboring. maybe take some moments to feel into your wants, let them flow onto a piece of paper or type them into an email response, be witnessed in them? sharing these parcels of our selfhood make for such magical moments of connection.

anyways, i left my writing in the space without taking any documentation, so i’ll have to try and remember what i said in that moment. in doing so, i’m feeling into how the desire within me has shifted since then. where is it now, sitting in the corner seat of a cafe, beanie warming my ears, hips open from morning yoga, carrying the energies of my weekend into another frigid day? we are, ultimately, vessels of translation. here, now, i translate my wants to your email inbox:
