in june, i’m teaching a seminar called [The Party](https://www.schoolofattention.org/enroll/p/party). maybe you want to sign up? come think about parties, study parties, practice partying with me 𓆑

i’ve been throwing parties for a while now, and keep getting more ambitious/elaborate/absurd. i feel as though i’m at a good inflection point to reflect and grow (through teaching!).

![pic from witch coven party](https://d2w9rnfcy7mm78.cloudfront.net/45948728/original_4a3f9ca6320e7c31853a1dd84754d878.png?1778254949?bc=0)
*from The Coven Seeks New Talent (Oct 2025)*

teaching feels to me like a true life hack (when did that phrase become commonplace?). there’s no faster way to learn something. last weekend, i led two leaf workshops in prospect park, which forced me to quickly elevate my leaf-knowledge from that of a casual appreciator to a serious student. i felt miles more confident in my ability to discern leaves, identify trees, and attend to leafing out after the workshops. the power of teaching!

i hope The Party does the same. my thesis is that parties are one of the most commonplace yet understudied human technologies. parties are social glue, movement fuel, solidarity-builders, conflict-generators, displays of wealth and popularity. they’re also creative mediums; i think of the party as an art practice, a way of gathering that asks *what might happen?* core to parties are emergence — we don’t know what’s going to happen. often, that’s why we party in the first place.

![pic from flower moon](https://d2w9rnfcy7mm78.cloudfront.net/45948775/original_8721e22afb7d73c79af10efad3639909.png?1778255062?bc=0)
*from the Flower Moon party (May 2026)*

the seminar will ask these questions and others, while also bringing us to practice — i want everyone involved to partake in imagining their own party or party series via the course. connected to this is a dream i’ve been nurturing of a party collective — like an art collective, but with parties as the medium of choice. essentially it would be like a group where we each support each others’ parties, from the big to the intimate, co-creating and imagining together. one of my favorite parts of living is those moments of creative flow with another, in which you’re both tripping over your words a bit to offer some new direction. parties are an exercise in radical imagination, a constant asking *what if?* i think treating them seriously as object of study and art practice is actually essential to building the bridges that are eroded by alienation.

anyways, if anyone ever wants to talk parties / trade feedback on party ideas, i’m always available. i actually don’t have any parties currently scheduled for the rest of the month (shockingly…) but perhaps one will emerge from these conversations/thinking. cheers!

kyle
