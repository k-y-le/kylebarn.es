good morning, slow morning, sunday morning (monday morning)

![good morning snail anime meme](https://d2w9rnfcy7mm78.cloudfront.net/40037782/original_eb55b580fef3696ad64324b9fa874c17.jpg?1759444148?bc=0)

i’m thinking about shapes today. saw a new tree outside my monday regular cafe spot, made banter with the barista. containers — that’s the word, been circling around it for years now. at the organizer lunch, i lead a meditation about anticipation, excitement, eagerness, containers too. as she leaves, lydia tells me “thank you for changing my life” and i return to my conversation. a little sprinkle of bewilderment, i think, is all we need.

the tree is one of those ones with deep vaselike purple flowers, something that seems more at home in california than here feeding off the gowanus canal seepage. something of a georgia o’keefe painting next to the construction — new high rise, new neighborhood. how old is a tree? hard to tell, but something tells me it’s always older, but also they choose the fast growers to put in cities, bring them over from iowa.

we do this, us humans, we shape the ambiguity. nick says “same arc as covid but 1 week” — like babies with that shape toy, learning squares and circles and triangles once again. who remembers trigonometry? soh cah toa, and i’m the first one to get to 23 adding numbers in the greenpoint early evening.

*pacific coast: land of purple flowers, logs, tide pools*

i’m realizing my future is the midden. soft pulp, decay, compost, a theme since i let the mushrooms take over on monday at nancy’s. here’s jeff vandermeer: “that’s how the madness of the world tries to colonize you: from the outside in, forcing you to live in its reality.” he calls in to consumption book club, tells us that the best tide pools are on the oregon coast. i haven’t been there, but heard there’s great rocks, jeff says he saw a mountain lion stranded on one before, caught by the high tide, and aren’t we all? tidal beings, caught by the sway, cast aside in cups of ocean swell?

monday evening drawing a timeline to the african copperbelt on a block in crown heights. great rocks in central park, i’m reminded, celebrating vayne’s exit from grad school. i’m bringing pierogi to the tamil new year potluck, so i carry two pounds of sauerkraut with me to the park. the dappled light illuminates a swan, petals dance and i inhale the wet forest smell. i text my landlord — broken dryer, still no lease for the rest of the month, but i already paid. last weekend i met two people from salem, oregon, which is much less witchy than the massachusetts one, they assure me, but it seems to me the magic must be on the coast, the rocky tide pools, the mountain lion pacing back and forth waiting for the retreat of the sea.

i’m making nests, making middens. look closely and you’ll see i’m ringed by satellites of ephemera, shimmering moons. muñoz calls ephemera “traces, glimmers, residues, and specks of things.” mostly, i just have so many zines, at this rate i’ll be starting a library in a few years, and why not? seems to me that archiving is one of the hot words in art lately, as if we haven’t been archiving forever, tossing another satellite into the midden.

i pick up a new red hat at the clothing swap, ask “is it giving maga?” we live to recognize patterns. make shapes. put that on a t-shirt, or a hat, but don’t use the wrong font or shade of red. i grab a jpegmafia shirt too, at the swap, even though i’ve never seen him live i like his music at least, conceptually too, i fuck with what he’s doing, even though i know wearing it will put me in a category with other white guys, sheepishly admitting they love stalker (1979) over dinner.

bewilderment, like when i’m covered in inchworms, skipping the breakout session of the save democracy conference. they seem to have no idea i’m not the best path to a leaf, yet still they diligently inch along, groping towards something terrible or divine. seems to me we’re playing different odds; what if i were one of fifty? playing the numbers game, if we’re lucky a few will make it to adulthood, make their mark on the world, spread the word and spread the seed, see what sticks, soft pulp within the oceanic feeling.

here’s jack halberstam: “while the arc of modern queer histories has bent toward legibility, recognition, maturity, and mutuality, wild bodies plot a different course through history and appear only at the very edge of definition, flickering in and out of meaning and sense and tending toward bewilderment.”at the tree identification workshop, two people are wearing frog and toad shirts; i don’t think they know each other. that used to be my avatar for a while, in my gamer days, my past life. these days the coming out is usually as a sports person, containing my excitement for the world cup. this too, a container makes.

shimmer here, alexis shotwell quoting mel chen: “a kind of shimmering presence… less easily bound to capital or to any other regimented time; or perhaps we could say that the time of capital is also no longer in the form it might once have been.” she’s invoking frog and toad here, sort of. talking about toxicity, waste, who we toss into the midden and why. a childrens book makes a spacious container, if you really spend time with it, we’re always filling it with what we wish.

all landscapes are memories, someone tells me. or maybe i’m hallucinating, lingering mushroom or the corn milk with a green tinge. i’m not sure anymore. my word of the year is whatever, anyways, so i’ll just let it lie, bewilder. making middens of it all.
