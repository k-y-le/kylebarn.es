yesterday, cammie and i sat drinking chamomile hyssop rooibos tea talking about the anatomy of cringe. what do we mean when we call something cringe? it’s about gaps, we think, between effort and reality, being un-self-aware, like the gimmick: “we call things gimmicks when it becomes radically uncertain if they are working too hard or too little, if they are historically backward or just as problematically advanced, if they are wonders or tricks.” (sianne ngai)

anyways, this morning i took a break from the busy coffee shop to look at flower buds. the tree basins in san francisco are so full of succulents and flowering plants. it feels a bit unfair, after months of brutal new york winter. but after a few minutes of looking at the details of a particularly alluring red flowering bush, i’m convinced that the best part of a flower is actually it’s buds. all these transitional states — on the way, a promise, a future foretold.

in honolulu last week, keely and i would end our days by sharing our rose/bud/thorn. each of these indexes a certain emotion: gratitude/excitement/displeasure. the thorn was the hardest to come up with, at times. i am an optimist at heart.

here’s thich nhat hanh: “if you see a flower only as a flower and don’t see the sunshine, clouds, earth, time, and space in it, you are caught in the sign of the flower. but when you have touched the nature of interbeing of the flower, you truly see the flower.” a word that keeps coming up is sincerity. sometimes, i feel like the purpose of my life is to spend time with sincere people. earnestly weird — that’s the vibe. it’s also kind of cringe; to be cringe is to be free.

continually applying for things can be kind of humiliating. like damn, i gotta prove myself *again?* but i keep doing it, because i’m a bud-thinker. i can’t stop dreaming of better futures. can’t stop turning everything into a project, because everything *can* be a project. after staring at the flower buds for a few minutes i took a bread to stand on a bike rack in the sun, drawing stares from children riding in bike trailers. i feel like this spider, not only because my long limbs seem even longer when standing a few feet above the sidewalk, and because i’m emerging from the flowers. also, we don’t think of pairing flowers and spiders together. actually, spiders are mostly eating the things that we associate with flowers. well, sometimes.

did you know that approximately 70% of bees live underground, not in hives? they’re mostly solitary. we keep telling these stories, imagining hexagonal tesselations coating the earth. save the bees! actually, the solitary ground bees are essential pollinators, and they prefer sparsely vegetated, dry soil. once again, lawns are eating the earth.

last year for easter i led an egg tour of manhattan. this year i’ll be in a park in san francisco giving away eggs and talking to people about their relationship with AI. i’m thinking about still doing the egg tour, even almost proposed for a walking festival in early may. but then i decided to do a leaf walk, instead, because i’m just so excited about leafing out. it’s like the buds, but for leaves, which i feel a kinship with because they are the less flashy yet just-as-essential part of the tree’s life story. eggs are like buds too, i think.

chamomile is a bud. so are capers, broccoli, artichokes, cloves. i don’t really know how i ended up writing about flower buds. i thought i was going to get into cringe, but maybe that will come later. one of the things that i enjoy about writing is surprising myself with where i’m headed. sometimes i’ll just put a few things together and then end up with a new connection. like an emergent network.

anyways, gonna go crawl out of a flower now (go to park and lay in sun. think about the end of the world and also should i get a snack). love u all <3
